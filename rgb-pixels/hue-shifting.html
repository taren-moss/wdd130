Hue Shifting is a very important skill for pixel art. It is the practice of shifting a color's hue in a palette (especially when shading). Generally, a color will be shifted towards a warmer hue as it gets lighter, and shifted towards a cooler hue as it gets darker. This makes the shadows look cooler and the highlights look warmer (which is what you'd expect from light). There are a couple exceptions to this generalized rule. For example, when hue-shifting with yellow, you can go towards green or orange for the darker shades, while for lighter shades you'd usually want to go closer to white (no hue is lighter than yellow). A similar rule applies to cyan - darker shades go towards blue, but lighter shades match white better than they match green.

Like any other art, these guidelines are only a recommendation and do not need to be followed exactly as I explain on this page. You could reverse the direction of the shift to get an unnatural look, or you could increase/reduce the shift to better fit the look you're going for. Regardless of how you use it, Hue Shifting gives your palette a lot more depth and vibrancy, and ultimately results in a nicer image.

  • Red shifts towards magenta or orange
  • Orange shifts towards red or yellow
  • Yellow shifts towards orange or white
  • Green shifts towards cyan or yellow
  • Cyan shifts towards blue or white
  • Blue shifts towards purple or cyan
  • Purple shifts towards blue or magenta
  • Magenta shifts towards purple or red
